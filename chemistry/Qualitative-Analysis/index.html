    # Cations

    1. Silver nitrate 0.2M
    2. Mercury (I) nitrate, 0.2M
    3. Copper (II) nitrate, 0.2M
    4. Zinc nitrate, 0.2M
    5. Cobalt (II) nitrate, 0.2M
    6. Lead nitrate, 0.5M
    7. Iron (III) nitrate, 0.2M
    8. Manganese (II) nitrate, 0.2M
    9. Aluminum nitrate, 0.2M
    10. Bismuth (III) nitrate, 0.2M

    # Reagents

    1. Hydrochloric acid, 6M
    2. Sulfuric acid, 6M
    3. Nitric acid, 6M
    4. Acetic acid, 6M
    5. Ammonia, 6M
    6. Sodium hydroxide, 6M
    7. Hydrogen peroxide, 3%
    8. Potassium thiocyanate, 0.1M
    9. Tin (II) chloride, 0.1M
    10. Aluminon solution, 0.1%
    11. Sodium bismuthate, solid
    12. Potassium nitrite, solid
    13. Potassium hexacyanoferrate (II), 0.1M

    # Steps

    # 1. Separation of Silver Group

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    1All ions$Hg_2Cl_2(s)$, $AgCl(s)$, $PbCl_2(s)$
    2Empty$Mn^{2+}$, $Co^{2+}$, $Fe^{3+}$, $Bi^{3+}$, $Cu^{2+}$, $Al^{3+}$, $Zn^{2+}$
    • add 8 drops of 6 M HCl to Tube 1
    • centrifuge any precipitants (Ag, Hg, Pb chlorides)
    • decant supernatant liquid into Tube 2
    • wash precipitate with water, then centrifuge and the washing fluid
    • If no precipitates form there are no silver group ions

    # 2. Separation and Confirmation of Lead

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    1$Hg_2Cl_2(s)$, $AgCl(s)$, $PbCl_2(s)$$Hg_2Cl_2(s)$, $AgCl(s)$
    ContainerEmpty$PbSO_4(s)$Dispose
    • add 1 mL distilled water to precipitates in Tube 1
    • heat tube in a boiling water bath
    • centrifuge, then decant liquid into separate container
    • add 5 drops of 6 M sulfuric acid to container
    • if a cloud appears, lead is present
    • repeat heating and decant until there is no more lead left in Tube 1 (cloud does not appear anymore)
    • dispose of any lead in container

    # 3. Separation of Mercury

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    1$Hg_2Cl_2(s)$, $AgCl(s)$$Hg(l)$, $HgNH_2Cl(s)$Dispose
    3Empty$Ag(NH_3)_2^+$
    • add 1 ml 6 M NH3 to Tube 1
    • silver should turn into colorless amine complex
    • if a dark gray precipitate forms, mercury is present
    • centrifuge, then transfer solution to Tube 3 dispose of any mercury in Tube 1

    # 4. Confirmation of Silver

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    3$Ag(NH_3)_2^+$$AgCl(s)$Dispose
    • add 15 drops 6 M HCl to Tube 3, until acidic
    • if white precipitate reappears, silver is present
    • dispose of any silver in Tube 3

    # 5. Separation of Aluminum and Zinc

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    2$Mn^{2+}$, $Co^{2+}$, $Fe^{3+}$, $Bi^{3+}$, $Cu^{2+}$, $Al^{3+}$, $Zn^{2+}$$MnO_2$, $Co(OH)_3(s)$, $Fe(OH)_3(s)$, $Bi(OH)_3(s)$, $Cu(OH)_2(s)$
    4Empty$Al(OH)_4^-$, $Zn(OH)_4^{2-}$
    • add 10 drops 3% hydrogen peroxide to Tube 2
    • add 6 M sodium hydroxide to Tube 2 until basic then add 3 more drops
    • record color of precipitate (if exists)
    • centrifuge solution, then decant supernatant fluid to Tube 4
    • wash precipitate in Tube 2 with 10 drops NaOH and 10 drops water

    # 6. Separation of Manganese and Cobalt

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    2Assorted Precipitates$MnO_2$, $Co(OH)_3$
    5Empty$Fe^{3+}$, $Cu^{2+}$, $Bi^{3+}$
    • add 5 drops water to Tube 2, then 6 M sulfuric acid until acidic (~6 drops)
    • centrifuge solution, then decant supernatant fluid to Tube 5
    • record color of precipitate and solution
    • wash precipitate in Tube 2 with water

    # 7. Dissolution of Manganese and Cobalt

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    2$MnO_2$, $Co(OH)_3$$Mn^{2+}$, $Co^{2+}$
    6Empty$Mn^{2+}$, $Co^{2+}$
    • add 1 mL water and 1mL sulfuric acid to Tube 2
    • add 1 mL 3% hydrogen peroxide, then heat in boiling water bath
    • after it is fully dissolved, pour half solution into Tube 6

    # 8. Confirmation of Manganese

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    2$Mn^{2+}$, $Co^{2+}$$MnO_4^-$, assorted ionsDispose
    • add 1 mL nitric acid and spatula of sodium bismuthate (until solid remains) to Tube 2
    • centrifuge, if the solution appears purple, manganese is present
    • dispose of contents in Tube 2

    # 9. Confirmation of Cobalt

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    6$Mn^{2+}$, $Co^{2+}$$K_3[Co(NO_2)_6](s)$, assorted ionsDispose
    • perform this step under a fume hood
    • add solid potassium nitrite to Tube 6
    • if a yellow precipitate forms, cobalt is present
    • dispose of contents in Tube 6

    # 10. Separation of Copper

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    5$Fe^{3+}$, $Cu^{2+}$, $Bi^{3+}$$Fe(OH)_3(s)$, $Bi(OH)_3(s)$
    ContainerEmpty$Cu(NH_3)_4^{2+}$, $Cu_2[Fe(CN)_6](s)$Dispose
    • add 6 M ammonia until solution is basic into Tube 5, then one more drop
    • centrifuge, then decant supernatant liquid into container
    • if the liquid appears blue, copper is present
    • for additional testing add 6M acetic acid until acidic
    • then add 2 drops potassium ferrocyanide
    • if a red-brown precipitate forms, copper is present
    • dispose of contents in container

    # 11. Confirmation of Iron

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    5$Fe(OH)_3(s)$, $Bi(OH)_3(s)$$FeSCN^{2+}$, assorted ionsDispose
    7Empty$Bi^{3+}$, $Fe^{3+}$
    • wash hydroxides in Tube 5
    • add 6M sulfuric acid dropwise until precipitate dissolves
    • pour half solution into Tube 7
    • add 5 drops 0.1M KSCN
    • if deep wine-red liquid forms, iron is present
    • dispose of contents in Tube 5

    # 12. Confirmation of Bismuth

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    7$Bi^{3+}$, $Fe^{3+}$$Bi(s)$, assorted ionsDispose
    ContainerEmpty$BiOCl(s)$Dispose
    • add 2 drops 6 M HCl to Tube 7
    • add 2-3 drops of this mixture to 300 mL tap water in container
    • if a white cloudiness forms, bismuth is present
    • add 6M NaOH to Tube 7 until precipitate forms, then add 10 more drops
    • add 5 drops 0.1M stannous chloride
    • if a black precipitate forms, bismuth is present
    • dispose of contents in container and Tube 7

    # 13. Separation of Zinc and Aluminum

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    4$Al(OH)_4^-$, $Zn(OH)_4^{2-}$$Al(OH)_3(s)$
    8Empty$Zn(NH_3)_4^{2+}(aq)$
    • add 10 drops of 6M nitric acid to Tube 4, continue until acidic
    • add 6M ammonia dropwise until basic, then 3 more drops
    • stir, centrifuge, decant liquid into Tube 8

    # 14. Confirmation of Aluminum

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
    4$Al(OH)_3(s)$red $Al(OH)_3(s)$Dispose
    • dissolve precipitate in Tube 4 in 6M HCl
    • add 3 drops aluminon soln, and 6M ammonia until basic
    • centrifuge
    • if a red precipitate remains, aluminum is present
    • dispose of contents in Tube 4

    # 15. Confirmation of Zinc

    Test TubeBeforeAfterNotes
